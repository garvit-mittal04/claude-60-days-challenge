1 · Executive Summary

OpsBrief is a morning briefing tool that connects to an SMB's Sheets, Snowflake, Looker, and Slack and writes the analyst's daily standup before they open their laptop. The Day 22 validation produced a Founder-Market Fit score of 78/100 and a conditional GO. This blueprint operationalizes that GO into a 30-day, no-code, no-platform MVP designed to prove a single hypothesis: that an SMB analyst's manager will pay $20/month for an automated morning brief delivered to Slack. If the hypothesis fails at 30 days, the founder kills or pivots — no sunk platform cost.

3 · Buyer Persona

"Maya — The Lone Analyst" (Primary user)

28 years old. Operations Analyst at a 180-person logistics SaaS. Three years out of a Business Analytics master's. Reports to a VP of Operations who joined from McKinsey and expects a Slack-message briefing every business day by 9 a.m. ET. Owns 4 dashboards in Hex, 7 sheets in Google Drive, a Snowflake read connection, and a Slack channel called #daily-ops. Spends her first 60–90 minutes opening tabs, copying numbers, and writing the same sentence structure she wrote yesterday.

What she wants: the next promotion. What's in her way: she does not have time to do analysis, only reporting. Pays via: personal/team card (no procurement gate at $20–$40/mo).

"Daniel — The Briefed Manager" (Secondary buyer)

42 years old. VP of Operations or Head of Finance. Reads the morning brief on his phone before his first meeting. Doesn't care how it gets written — cares that it's correct, on time, and the same format every day. Approves the $40/mo charge if Maya brings it to him with one sentence: "this saves me an hour a day."

6a · Key Customer Objections

  • "Can't we just use ChatGPT on our Sheets?" → Counter: cross-source, scheduled, with anomaly memory across days.
  • "You'll have access to our financial data." → Counter: read-only connectors, customer-controlled scope, SOC 2 on Q3 roadmap.
  • "What if the AI hallucinates a number?" → Counter: every figure links back to the source row; the brief is summary, not synthesis.
  • "My analyst will see this as a threat." → Counter: gives them time back for higher-leverage work, not replaces.
  • "We already pay for Hex/Looker." → Counter: OpsBrief sits on top, not instead. $20–$40 increment.

6b · Key Buying Triggers

  • New manager joins, asks "where's my morning brief?"
  • Star analyst leaves; manager realizes the brief was 100% in their head.
  • Quarterly all-hands prep — manager wants a clean 90-day summary.
  • An anomaly got missed → "this shouldn't happen again" mandate from leadership.
  • Analyst is on the edge of burnout/notice — retention manager pays $40/mo to make their day easier.

7 · MVP Recommendation

What to build first

A Google Apps Script + Claude API + Slack webhook that reads one Google Sheet for one customer, generates a 6-sentence morning brief, and posts it to a designated Slack channel at 8:55 a.m. each business day. 5 lines of code. Zero front-end.

What NOT to build

No login. No website. No connector marketplace. No multi-tenant database. No admin dashboard. No mobile app. No team-management UI. No SSO. No pricing page. No "Plans" tab. Nothing that doesn't directly contribute to a brief landing in Slack at 8:55 a.m.

Success metrics

  • ≥5 paid customers at $20/mo by Day 30
  • ≥80% morning open rate on the Slack brief
  • ≥3 customers explicitly forward the brief to their manager
  • ≥1 unsolicited referral by Day 30

10 · Top 5 Risks

HIGH

Willingness-to-pay at $20/mo

Day 22 SOM math assumes $20–$40 ACV. Reality may be $0 — analysts may refuse to pay personally, and managers may say "we already pay for Hex." 30-day test answers this.

HIGH

Hallucinated metric in a CFO brief

One wrong number sent to a real CFO and the product dies in that account. Source-link-back design is non-negotiable from Day 1.

MED

Incumbent wedge closure

Google Sheets + Gemini, Hex Magic, Tableau Pulse closing the gap. Estimated window: 18–24 months. Must move on the no-code MVP within Q3 2026.

MED

F-1 visa monetization constraint

Founder cannot legally take revenue from a US-incorporated startup as F-1 student. Mitigation: defer revenue to OPT period or partner with US co-founder. Must consult UTD ISS before charging.

LOW

Discovery-call channel

Reaching 25 SMB analysts in 30 days requires LinkedIn outreach discipline. Founder is already posting daily — pipeline is warm. Low risk if framing is right.

11 · 30-Day MVP Plan

Week 1 · Days 1–7Sharpen the wedge
  • Reach out to 20 SMB analysts in your LinkedIn network for 25-min discovery calls
  • Identify 50 target ICP companies via LinkedIn Sales Navigator
  • Build a 5-question discovery script focused on the 90-minute morning routine
  • Spin up a single-page Carrd site: "Your morning brief, automated. $20/mo." Email collection only.
Week 2 · Days 8–14Run 8 discovery calls
  • Complete 8 discovery calls; transcribe and code answers in a sheet
  • Identify the top 3 most-painful sub-segments (e-commerce ops? Healthcare ops? SaaS finance?)
  • Post 2 LinkedIn observations from the calls; collect inbound interest in DMs
  • Iterate the discovery script based on what's resonating
Week 3 · Days 15–21Build the no-code MVP
  • Wire Google Apps Script + Claude API + Slack webhook for ONE pilot customer
  • Deliver the first real morning brief Tuesday at 8:55 a.m.
  • Iterate the brief format with that single customer for 5 consecutive workdays
  • Document the prompt template and the brief schema as the productized v1
Week 4 · Days 22–30Convert to first paying users
  • Run 8 more discovery calls focused on validated sub-segments
  • Pitch the no-code MVP to 10 warm prospects at $20/mo (free 7-day trial)
  • Target: 5 paying customers signed by Day 30
  • Decision point at Day 30: ≥5 paid → continue; <5 paid → pivot to consumer-of-one or kill
