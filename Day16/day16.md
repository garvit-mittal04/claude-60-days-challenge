## Key Learnings

**1. Skills convert a 1,000-word prompt into a 2-word command.** Before today, every stock analysis required pasting the full ~600-word prompt template. With the Skill saved, every future analysis is a single sentence — *"Analyze SNOW,"* *"Compare SNOW vs MDB,"* *"Pros & Cons of NVDA."* The 30-minute setup pays back on the first reuse.

**2. The real value is the rules — not the format.** The Skill's most useful component isn't the output structure; it's the *guardrails* — no buy/sell/hold calls, mandatory source citations, "data unavailable" flag when retrieval fails, no fabricated figures. These rules are easy to forget when prompting freshly each time. The Skill enforces them every single invocation.

**3. Adaptation > rewriting.** The original template targeted Indian NSE/BSE stocks. Instead of writing a new Skill, I extended the source priority to include US sources (stockanalysis.com, SEC, Finviz). Same Skill, broader applicability. Skills are forks, not finals.

**4. Skills compound across the 60-day journey.** Day 11 ATS analysis → Day 13 Indeed market data → Day 16 financial deep-dive on the company driving that market gap. Each day's tool stacks on the previous. The compound is the value.

**5. The "permanent capability" framing changes how I prompt.** When prompting fresh, I optimize for *this one chat*. When designing a Skill, I optimize for *every future chat*. The mental shift forces sharper rules, more constraints, and better guardrails — produces a higher-quality output template than any one-off prompt would.

**6. Reading the company you're learning the tool of is double leverage.** Knowing Snowflake the *product* and Snowflake the *business* are two different skills. As a future Business Analyst, the second skill differentiates you in interviews — "I use Snowflake AND I understand its competitive position vs Databricks" lands harder than just listing the tool on a resume.

## What surprised me most

The Snowflake fundamentals genuinely surprised me. The narrative I'd absorbed was "high-growth, burning cash, AI hype-cycle." The actual data shows a company that just generated $1.17B in TTM free cash flow with 67% gross margins and 29% revenue growth at $5B scale. The "growth-stage cash burner" framing is 2 years out of date — but the public narrative hasn't caught up.

That gap — between what the market *thinks* the data says and what the data *actually* says — is the entire job of a fundamental analyst. The Skill makes it 10x faster to find that gap on any stock in any sector.
