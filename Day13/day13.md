## Market Demand Insights

**What the market is signaling right now:**

1. **Remote is still alive at the analyst level — but compressed.** Roughly 60% of top matches were Remote or Remote-friendly. Comp at the high end (>$130K) is rare for remote BA/DA roles; most cluster in the $75–110K band.

2. **"Business Analyst" is the highest-leverage title for your profile.** Pure "Data Scientist" or "ML Engineer" filtered out — too senior, too SWE-heavy. Pure "Financial Analyst" undersold your AI work. Business Analyst (or "BA II" / "Senior BA") splits the middle and matches both your Finance + AI sides.

3. **Fintech and SaaS dominate the strongest-fit listings.** Smartsheet, Salted, Zeta Global, Interchecks, EARNEST, Avalara — all software-first. Your Streamlit deployments + Python + dashboarding pattern-match exactly what these teams hire for.

4. **Senior FP&A is overheated; entry FP&A is undervalued.** Compensation jumps massively from "FP&A Analyst" ($50–80K) to "Senior FP&A Analyst" ($100–145K). Implication: 2 years post-grad in any FP&A seat → significant comp jump.

5. **"AI" in the JD title now mostly means contract work.** "AI Strategy Analyst" and "AI Trainer" roles trend gig / project-based. Career-track AI work appears under non-"AI" titles like "Data Analyst, Data Cloud Intelligence" (Zeta) or "Sr. Financial Analyst R&D FP&A" (Smartsheet).

6. **Defense/aerospace is paying but mismatched.** Raytheon and Northrop Grumman appeared with $86–165K bands, but cleared / clearance-track work doesn't align with your profile and excludes non-US-citizens from many roles.

## Key Learnings

**1. Live job-search APIs change the game.** Instead of guessing what the market wants, Claude pulled 30+ live JDs in 4 parallel searches. The "what are the actual required skills" question was answered with frequency-counted data, not vibes.

**2. The top-of-funnel filter is title, not skill.** "Business Analyst" pulls 5x the relevant volume of "Data Scientist" for my profile. Picking the right top-of-funnel keyword changes what reaches you by 10x.

**3. Compensation bands reveal hiring level.** A wide band like $74K–$137K (Thomson Reuters) means the company is willing to hire across levels. A narrow band $50K–$65K means they're targeting one tier. Wide bands = better odds for an early-career candidate to slip in.

**4. The cloud gap is the universal gap.** ~50% of my top matches require Snowflake / AWS in some form. This isn't a "nice to have" anymore — it's the new "SQL." One weekend cert closes the gap on hundreds of postings.

**5. "Senior" titles are noisier than the actual seniority required.** Smartsheet listed "Sr. Financial Analyst" with a $92K starting point — that's not senior comp. The title is calibrated for the team's culture, not the candidate's experience. Apply anyway.

**6. The AI title is a trap.** Roles with "AI" in the title cluster around contract / gig work. Real AI-adjacent career roles use plain titles like "Data Analyst" and "Financial Analyst" — the work itself involves ML and modern data stacks.
