🛰️ How the Control Tower works

You are the Head of Operations for a global supply chain company. For the next 3 minutes, alerts will appear on your dashboard. Your job is to triage them — fast — and pick the action that keeps customer satisfaction, service level, and revenue protected high.

How alerts work

  • Each alert has a priority (Critical / High / Medium / Low) and a time remaining bar.
  • If you don't act before time runs out, the alert auto-fails and your KPIs take damage.
  • Each alert offers 4 actions. One is usually the recommended response — the rest carry consequences.

KPIs you're protecting

  • Service Level % — orders fulfilled on time
  • Customer Satisfaction — NPS-style score
  • Inventory Health — stock balance
  • Transportation Efficiency — how well your logistics flows
  • Operating Cost — lower is better
  • Revenue Protected — dollars saved from disruption

Difficulty

Alerts arrive more frequently as time progresses. The final minute is the hardest — that's where great operators are separated from average ones.
